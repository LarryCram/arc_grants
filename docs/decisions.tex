\section{Design Decisions Log}
\label{sec:decisions}

This document records key methodological decisions, the evidence or reasoning
behind them, and alternatives considered. It serves as the methodology
defence for the scholarly paper and policy report.

\subsection{2018 ANZSRC FoR Code Revision}
The 2018 revision of the Australian and New Zealand Standard Research
Classification introduced discontinuities in Field of Research codes that
make direct pre/post comparison unreliable at the 4- and 6-digit level.
\textbf{Decision:} FoR codes are used at 2-digit division level only for
cross-period comparisons. Co-investigator overlap is the primary bridge
signal spanning the discontinuity.

\subsection{Precision Over Recall}
In investigator disambiguation, a false merge (two people treated as one)
creates systematic upward bias in productivity measures in exactly the
window of interest. \textbf{Decision:} Where confidence is insufficient,
records are flagged for manual review rather than merged automatically.

\subsection{ORCID Treatment}
ORCID coverage is approximately 30\% and contains both errors of omission
and commission. \textbf{Decision:} ORCIDs are treated as hints requiring
validation, not authoritative identifiers.
